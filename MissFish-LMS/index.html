HACCP Principles
Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points

HACCP is a systematic preventive approach to food safety that addresses physical, chemical, and biological hazards as a means of prevention rather than finished product inspection.

💡 HACCP was developed in the 1960s for NASA's space food program and is now a globally recognised food safety standard required by Miss Fish for all kitchen staff.
The 7 HACCP Principles
  1. Conduct a hazard analysis
  2. Identify Critical Control Points (CCPs)
  3. Establish critical limits for each CCP
  4. Establish monitoring procedures
  5. Establish corrective actions
  6. Establish verification procedures
  7. Establish record-keeping and documentation
Critical Control Points at Miss Fish

At Miss Fish, our key CCPs include: receiving raw ingredients, cold storage temperatures, cooking temperatures, hot holding, and cooling procedures. Each must be logged in our daily food safety checklist.

HACCP Principles
Hazard Analysis & Critical Control Points

HACCP is a systematic preventive approach to food safety that addresses physical, chemical, and biological hazards as a means of prevention rather than inspection.

💡 Required for all Miss Fish kitchen and food handling staff. Completion is mandatory before handling guest orders.
The 7 HACCP Principles
  1. Conduct a hazard analysis
  2. Identify Critical Control Points
  3. Establish critical limits
  4. Establish monitoring procedures
  5. Establish corrective actions
  6. Establish verification procedures
  7. Record-keeping and documentation
